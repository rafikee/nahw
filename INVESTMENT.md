# Nahw (نَحْو) — Investment One-Pager

**One-liner.** Nahw is a mobile-first, Arabic-native app that turns the classical Arabic-grammar curriculum into a Duolingo-style interactive course — built for the heritage and Muslim learner the global edtech players ignore.

## Vision

A world where any heritage speaker, student, or adult Muslim can reach functional fluency in classical Arabic grammar (نَحْو) from their phone — the way Duolingo did for Spanish, but for the language of the Qur'an and 1,400 years of scholarship.

## Mission

Take a respected, time-tested grammar curriculum and rebuild it as a structured, interactive, mobile course with full tashkeel, faithful pedagogy, and a quality bar that traditional teachers will endorse.

## The problem

Today's options are bimodal:

- **Textbooks** (الآجرومية, النحو الواضح, مدينة) — authoritative, but static, sequential, and unforgiving for self-learners.
- **Generalist apps** (Duolingo Arabic, Memrise) — gamified, but teach colloquial vocabulary, skip grammar, and treat Arabic as just another European language with letters reversed.
- **Online programs** (Bayyinah, Qutor, Madinah Arabic) — high quality, but mostly video lectures or 1:1 tutoring; expensive, passive, no interactive practice loop.

Nothing in the middle: serious grammar content delivered in a modern, self-paced, exercise-driven format.

## Audience

- **Primary:** Heritage Arabic speakers and students of Qur'anic Arabic — madrasah and seminary students, adult Muslims worldwide, converts, and second-generation diaspora who can read the script but never learned grammar formally.
- **Secondary:** Arabic teachers and Islamic schools who want a homework-grade companion app for their students.

The product is intentionally Arabic-first (UI, copy, examples — all in Arabic with full harakat). That is a feature, not a bug: it filters for the audience that will actually pay and stick.

## Product wedge

Faithful textbook content × Duolingo-style step flow × full تَشْكِيل × RTL-native × mobile-first. Each lesson runs intro → concept (definition + examples + quick-check MCQ) → review quiz → tap-to-classify word sort.

The defensible asset is the **AI-assisted authoring pipeline**: OCR'd source text + a structured JSON schema + agent prompts produce a polished lesson in hours, not weeks. One author can ship a curriculum that previously required a publishing team.

## Market opportunity

~400M Arabic speakers and ~2B Muslims worldwide carry a cultural or religious incentive to learn classical Arabic. Even a low single-digit penetration of the serious-learner segment is a multi-million-user TAM. Comparable verticals (Bayyinah Institute, Qutor) have proven willingness to pay for quality Arabic instruction at $20–$200/mo price points; no one has captured the self-paced app tier yet.

## Why now

1. **AI collapses content cost.** Producing a tashkeel-perfect grammar curriculum used to require scholars and editors. LLMs + a tight authoring spec turn it into a one-person operation.
2. **RTL/Arabic typography on the web finally works.** Noto Naskh, modern CSS, and mobile browsers render classical Arabic beautifully. Five years ago this app would have looked broken.
3. **Demand is up.** Post-pandemic, demand for self-paced Islamic and Arabic learning has surged; Bayyinah, Qabeelat, and Arabic-learning creators on YouTube/TikTok have warmed the audience.
4. **Distribution channels exist.** A network of established teachers and creators in this space gives a lean team a credible path to a first 10k users without paid acquisition.

## Business strategy

- **Free tier:** Book 1 (foundations) — full access, builds funnel and trust.
- **Subscription:** Full curriculum, spaced-repetition review, progress sync (~$5–10/mo).
- **B2B:** License to madrasahs and Islamic schools as a homework/companion tool (per-seat).
- **Adjacent expansion:** Reuse the authoring pipeline to ship صَرْف (morphology), بلاغة (rhetoric), and Qur'anic parsing courses on the same rails.

## Roadmap

- **Now (v0.1):** Polish Book 1 (4 lessons live), ship as installable PWA, instrument basic analytics.
- **3–6 months:** Books 2–3 covering remaining foundations and intro إعراب; spaced-repetition review; user accounts and cross-device sync; native iOS wrapper.
- **6–12 months:** Teacher dashboard, audio recitation of examples, Qur'anic-verse parsing exercises, first paid tier.
- **12 months+:** Adjacent verticals (صَرْف, بلاغة, عَرُوض), B2B pilot with one or two Islamic schools.

## Risks

Content-author bandwidth (mitigated by the AI pipeline), monetization timing (free tier must convert without alienating the community), and distribution dependence on a small set of creator/teacher channels.
